About Smart Card Frauds

Smart card frauds occur when attackers exploit stolen or cloned card details or manipulate transaction flows to illicitly transfer funds. Common patterns:

  • Card skimming/cloning: Theft of card data for remote transactions.
  • Unauthorized transfers: Sudden large transfers to new accounts.
  • Rapid sequence attacks: Multiple quick transactions in a short time.
  • Account takeover: Unusual changes in account balance patterns.

What is Smart Card Fraud?

Smart card fraud refers to the unauthorized use or manipulation of smart cards such as debit cards, credit cards, transit cards, or digital IDs. Even with secure chip technology, these cards can be compromised through cloning, PIN theft, tampered readers, or malicious software. Attackers may steal card data using skimming devices or trick users into revealing sensitive information. Lost or stolen cards can also be misused if authentication measures are weak. Some advanced techniques involve intercepting communication between the card and terminal. Such attacks lead to financial loss, identity theft, and system breaches. Continuous monitoring and machine learning-based detection help identify abnormal patterns and prevent fraudulent activity.
